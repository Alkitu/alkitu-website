## Content Format Recommendations

### For GEO (AI Visibility)

Every blog post should include:

1. **FAQ section** at the bottom (3-5 questions with direct answers)
   - These get picked up by ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews
   - Use the `getFaqSchema()` utility already in the codebase

2. **Statistics and citations** throughout
   - "According to [source], [specific stat]"
   - LLMs prioritize content with verifiable claims

3. **Comparison tables** where relevant
   - "Custom Code vs WordPress vs Shopify" table format
   - AI models extract structured comparisons easily

4. **Step-by-step processes**
   - Numbered steps with clear headings
   - LLMs love citing structured processes

5. **Definitions early in the post**
   - "Brand engineering is..." in the first paragraph
   - This is what gets pulled into AI-generated answers

### For SEO (Google Search)

1. **Title**: 50-60 characters, keyword at the front
2. **Meta description**: 155 characters, include a number or question
3. **H2 structure**: One H2 per major section, keyword variations
4. **Internal links**: Link to relevant service pages (branding, product-building, etc.)
5. **CTA**: Every post should link to contact or relevant service page
6. **Image**: Custom hero image with descriptive alt text

## Competitive Gaps to Exploit

### What US agencies DON'T talk about (your advantage):

1. **GEO as a service** — Almost no one in English is writing actionable content about optimizing for AI search. This is a 12-month window before it becomes mainstream.

2. **Brand engineering with ISO standards** — US agencies sell "branding packages" but never reference international standards. This positions Alkitu as more rigorous.

3. **Spec-Driven Development** — Most dev agencies talk about "agile." Spec-driven is a contrarian position that appeals to clients burned by scope creep.

4. **Design systems as deliverables** — US agencies deliver Figma files. Alkitu delivers systems. This is a meaningful differentiator for repeat business.

5. **Bilingual US + LATAM reach** — A US company with Spanish-speaking customers needs a bilingual partner. Very few agencies genuinely serve both markets.

## What NOT to Write About

These topics are saturated and won't rank, regardless of quality:

- "What is SEO" (HubSpot, Moz, Ahrefs own this forever)
- "Top 10 web design trends" (every design blog publishes this)
- "How to create a logo" (Canva, Looka, 99designs dominate)
- "Best website builders" (affiliate sites with massive DA own this)
- "Social media marketing tips" (too generic, no differentiation)
- "What is digital marketing" (Wikipedia, HubSpot, owned forever)
